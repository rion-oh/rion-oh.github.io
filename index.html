Research Interests

I am interested in understanding how galaxies form and evolve, with a focus on the physical processes that drive galaxy quenching. My work centers on the role of gas in this transition, particularly neutral gas outflows and the evolution of gas reservoirs in high-redshift galaxies.

Research Experience

Neutral Gas Outflows in Quenching Galaxies with JWST
University of Washington — Advisor: Arianna S. Long

  • Selected a quiescent galaxy sample at 2.5 < z < 6 from JWST NIRSpec data using Hα equivalent width
  • Performed SED fitting on 3,000+ galaxies using Prospector to model stellar continua
  • Stacked continuum-subtracted spectra to detect Mg II and Na I D absorption features
  • Derived outflow velocities and neutral gas column densities across redshift and galaxy type

Intracluster Gas Modeling in Galaxy Clusters
KAIST — Advisor: Junhan Kim

  • Modeled hot intracluster gas using joint X-ray and Sunyaev–Zel’dovich analysis
  • Implemented triaxial ellipsoidal models for gas density and pressure
  • Performed Bayesian inference using MCMC (emcee, redemcee)
